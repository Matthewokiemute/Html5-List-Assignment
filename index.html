BANGA SOUP RECIPE(DELTA VERSION)

Banga Soup is a very popular Nigerian Palm nut soup, it’s a Delta/Urhobo favourite. The preparation method of Banga soup varies from tribe to tribe. The Igbo version is called Ofe Akwu which includes the addition of Ugu or Scent leaves which is without in the Delta version. There’s also the Efik version called Abak Atama.

Ingredients needed for preparing Banga Soup

  • 1500grams Palm nut Extract
  • 1 Medium size Catfish or any preferred fresh fish
  • Assorted Meats of your choice
  • 2-3 Medium Pieces Stockfish (Panla, Okporoko)
  • 1-2 Medium Dried Fish ( I’m using catfish)
  • 1/2 Cup Fresh or Smoked Shrimps (Optional)
  • 1 Tablespoon Ground Dried Crayfish or prawns ( Prawns preferably)
  • 1-2 Tablespoons Banga Spice
  • 1-2 Tablespoons Crushed Obeletientien leaves or Dried bitter leaves.
  • 1 Oburunbebe stick
  • 2 Scotch bonnet ( Atarodo)
  • 1 Medium onion chopped
  • 2 Tablespoons dried pepper (Atagungun)
  • 1/2 Cup Periwinkles
  • Knorr cubes
  • Salt to taste

Preperation:

  1. Boil your meats first, if you’re using any. I’m using smoked turkey. Remember to start with the tougher meats, then add the softer ones as you go.
  2. When it’s tender, add the dried stockfish and the blended Scotch bonnet, continue to boil till the stockfish is tender and the meats/smoked turkey soft…
  3. Then, add the smoked fish, switch off the heat and simmer with the residual heat for 2-3 minutes…
  4. Now, transfer your palm nut extract into a big pot. If you’re using the tinned extract, it comes really thick, dilute with hot water, the water should be almost double the quantity of the extract. I’ll be using the stock from the meats as well, so, I’m including the quantity of the stock as part of the water I’m diluting the extract with.
  5. When you’ve added the water and stock, place on a hob on medium heat, mix thoroughly to break up clumps.
  6. Do not cover the pot from now till the end of the cooking process, Let this boil for 10-12 minutes…
  7. Then add the Banga Spice, Oburunbebe stick, ground crayfish or prawns, dried pepper, knorr cube and salt to taste. You may not need too much salt if your stock is well seasoned so be mindful.
  8. Now, add the Catfish or your preferred fresh fish and fresh shrimps, (now is a good time to add periwinkles if you’re using any), turn the heat down to low, leave to cook for 7-10 minutes.
  9. Blend your bitter leaves or Obeletientien…
  10. add the dried Obeletientien or bitter leaves, stir and combine…cook for 2 minutes, then switch off the heat.
  11. Leave to cook for a further 2-3 minutes with the residual heat
  12. Remove the Oburunbebe stick from the Soup
  13. And you’re done…
